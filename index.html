The 10-minute fix checklist

Use this immediately after your result. The goal is one measurable response improvement, not a redesign.

Step 1: One primary next step

Choose one action you want users to take (book, call, request a quote, buy). Remove competing actions above the fold.

Step 2: Put proof next to the decision

Add one specific proof item near the CTA (testimonial with outcome, metric, recognizable client type, before/after).

Step 3: Remove one friction point

Cut one step from the path (click, field, or choice). If the action feels easier, response climbs.

FAQ

Short answers, because you have work to do.

Is this telling me to post more?

No. Posting more can amplify a broken system. This diagnostic focuses on what happens after someone notices you.

Do I need to change my whole website?

Usually not. Most response issues come from one weak link: unclear next step, thin trust, or unnecessary friction.

Why call it “rizz” at all?

We use it as shorthand for response, not charisma. The goal is follow-through, not performance.

Will this work for local businesses?

Yes. “No inquiries from website” is one of the most common local-business problems. The mechanics are the same.

Rizzry focuses on response systems for solo operators. If your site gets attention but no action, the problem is rarely effort. It is the missing bridge between interest and the next step.
